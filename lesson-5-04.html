5.04 — Data Sets and Outliers

An outlier is a value way smaller or bigger than the rest. This whole quiz is about spotting outliers, knowing how they distort each statistic, and deciding what to report when they exist.

10 questions ~40 min Due Thursday

A. What's an outlier?

An outlier is a data point that's much smaller or larger than the rest of the data. Examples:

  • {2, 4, 4, 5, 5, 6, 50} — the 50 is way bigger → outlier.
  • {11, 85, 87, 88, 91, 91, 92, 93, 95} — the 11 is way smaller → outlier.

Outliers can come from typos, real one-off events, or measurement errors. Either way, they mess up the "typical" picture of the data.

B. Spotting outliers visually

On a box plot: outliers usually appear as isolated points OUTSIDE the whiskers (like dots floating beyond the min/max line).

outlier ● min Q1 med Q3 max outlier ● |──┌────|────┐──| (box around middle 50%)

On a dot plot: a lone dot far from the cluster.

On a histogram: a tiny bar far away from the main bulk of bars.

C. The 1.5×IQR rule (formal definition)

To prove a value is an outlier (not just "looks weird"), use the IQR rule:

  1. Compute IQR = Q3 − Q1.
  2. Low cutoff = Q1 − 1.5 × IQR.
  3. High cutoff = Q3 + 1.5 × IQR.
  4. Anything below low or above high is an outlier.
Worked example

Data: 12, 15, 17, 18, 20, 22, 23, 25, 50. Identify outliers.

  1. Sort (done). Median (5th of 9) = 20.
  2. Lower half: 12, 15, 17, 18 → Q1 = (15+17)/2 = 16.
  3. Upper half: 22, 23, 25, 50 → Q3 = (23+25)/2 = 24.
  4. IQR = 24 − 16 = 8.
  5. Low cutoff = 16 − 1.5(8) = 16 − 12 = 4.
  6. High cutoff = 24 + 1.5(8) = 24 + 12 = 36.
  7. 50 > 36 → outlier.

D. Effect of outliers on each statistic — the cheat sheet

StatisticOutlier effectWhy
Mean🚨 Big effect (pulled toward outlier)Uses every value
Median✓ Barely affectedJust the middle value(s)
Range🚨 Big effectmax − min directly uses extremes
IQR✓ Barely affectedUses only Q1 and Q3
Standard Deviation🚨 Big effectSums squared distances from mean
Mode✓ Not affectedMost common value, outliers are rare

Memorize this table. It directly answers half the 5.04 questions.

E. Computing mean/median with vs. without outliers

Common question format: "What's the difference in the mean/median when you include vs. exclude the outlier?"

Worked example

Lattes sold: 55, 52, 56, 48, 57, 20, 45, 41. Difference in median with vs. without outlier (20)?

  1. WITH outlier (8 values), sorted: 20, 41, 45, 48, 52, 55, 56, 57. Median = (48+52)/2 = 50.
  2. WITHOUT outlier (7 values), sorted: 41, 45, 48, 52, 55, 56, 57. Median = 52.
  3. Difference = 52 − 50 = 2.
Pattern: Mean changes a lot when you remove an outlier. Median barely moves. If the outlier is HIGH, removing it makes the mean go DOWN. If the outlier is LOW, removing it makes the mean go UP.

F. Real-world decision-making

Questions about which employee/team/option is "better on average" — with outlier data — use these rules:

  • Higher median = better center. (Median ignores outlier noise.)
  • Smaller IQR = more consistent.
  • Don't choose based on min/max alone — those are just the extremes.

G. Practice Problems

Problem 1

A data set has Q1 = 10, Q3 = 30. Using the 1.5×IQR rule, which value is an outlier?

  • A. −15
  • B. 0
  • C. 45
  • D. 65
Show answer
D. IQR = 30 − 10 = 20. Low cutoff = 10 − 30 = −20. High cutoff = 30 + 30 = 60. 65 > 60 → outlier.
Problem 2

Test scores include outlier 72. With the outlier, mean = 86. When the outlier is removed, what's always true?

  • A. Mean increases
  • B. Mean decreases
  • C. Median increases
  • D. Median decreases
Show answer
A. Removing a LOW outlier (72) raises the mean. The remaining scores must average higher than 86 once 72 is gone.
Problem 3

A box plot shows a clear outlier dot far below the min. If the outlier is removed, which statistic is LEAST changed?

  • A. Mean
  • B. Range
  • C. Standard deviation
  • D. Median
Show answer
D. Median is robust to outliers. Mean, range, and σ all use extreme values directly.
Problem 4

Softball team sizes: 9, 12, 8, 7, 7, 21, 11, 9, 8, 7, 10, 7, 10, 11. Which statement is true?

  • A. One outlier — unusually LARGE
  • B. Two outliers — unusually LARGE
  • C. One outlier — unusually SMALL
  • D. Two outliers — unusually SMALL
Show answer
A. Most teams have 7-12 players. 21 stands out way above. (Check with 1.5×IQR rule: sorted = 7,7,7,7,8,8,9,9,10,10,11,11,12,21. Median = (9+10)/2 = 9.5. Q1 = 7.5, Q3 = 11. IQR = 3.5. High cutoff = 11 + 5.25 = 16.25. 21 > 16.25, outlier ✓.)
Problem 5

Lattes sold: 55, 52, 56, 48, 57, 20, 45, 41. What is the difference between the median including the outlier and excluding it?

  • A. 2
  • B. 3
  • C. 30
  • D. 52
Show answer
A. Sorted with outlier (8 values): 20,41,45,48,52,55,56,57. Median = (48+52)/2 = 50. Sorted without (7 values): 41,45,48,52,55,56,57. Median = 52. Difference = 52 − 50 = 2.
Problem 6

Tammy and Wyatt are sales associates. Tammy: median 25.5, IQR 5.75. Wyatt: median 24, IQR 7. Who should get the promotion based on "best on average"?

  • A. Wyatt — more evenly distributed
  • B. Wyatt — highest single day
  • C. Tammy — her lowest is higher
  • D. Tammy — higher median, smaller IQR
Show answer
D. Higher median = better center. Smaller IQR = more consistent. Tammy wins on both.
Problem 7

A dot plot: 15, 23, 24, 26, 27, 35. What's the effect of removing the outliers on the mean?

  • A. Remains the same
  • B. Decreases by 2
  • C. Increases by 2
  • D. There are no outliers
Show answer
A. Check with 1.5×IQR rule. Sorted: 15,23,24,26,27,35. Median = (24+26)/2 = 25. Q1 = 23, Q3 = 27. IQR = 4. Low = 23 − 6 = 17. High = 27 + 6 = 33. Both 15 and 35 are outliers, but they're roughly equidistant from the center so removing both barely changes the mean. Original mean = 150/6 = 25. Without: 100/4 = 25. Same!
Problem 8

If outliers are in the data, which pair of statistics best represents center and spread?

  • A. Mean + standard deviation
  • B. Mean + IQR
  • C. Median + standard deviation
  • D. Median + IQR
Show answer
D. Both robust to outliers. (This is the same pairing rule from 5.02.)
Problem 9

A box plot has an extreme outlier on the right. Which of the following increases significantly if the outlier is included?

  • A. Median
  • B. IQR
  • C. Mean
  • D. Q1
Show answer
C. Mean is dragged toward the outlier. Median, IQR, and Q1 are robust.
Problem 10

Two dot plots: Week 1 has ages 62-89. Week 2 has ages 62-89 PLUS 10 more golfers (similar range). True statement?

  • A. Range Week 1 = Range Week 2
  • B. Range Week 1 > Range Week 2
  • C. Median Week 1 < Median Week 2
  • D. Mean Week 1 > Mean Week 2
Show answer
A. Both have the same minimum (62) and same maximum (89), so the range = 89 − 62 = 27 for both. The medians/means may shift slightly depending on which ages were added but the range is unchanged.

H. What to do next

🎯 Key things to remember

  1. Effect table: Mean, Range, σ are pulled by outliers. Median, IQR, Mode are robust.
  2. 1.5×IQR rule: outlier if value < Q1 − 1.5·IQR or > Q3 + 1.5·IQR.
  3. Remove HIGH outlier → mean DROPS. Remove LOW outlier → mean RISES.
  4. Median moves slightly when you remove an outlier (by one shift), unlike the mean.
  5. "Better on average" = higher MEDIAN, smaller IQR. Don't use min/max alone.
