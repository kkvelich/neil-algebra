5.02 — Comparing Data Sets

This is the hardest quiz so far. It's about choosing the right statistical tool — not just computing. Mean or median? Standard deviation or IQR? When does a normal distribution rule kick in?

A. Symmetric vs. Skewed Distributions

Before picking a stat, you need to know the shape of the data. Three main shapes:

  • Symmetric — data balanced around the middle. Mean ≈ Median.
  • Skewed right — long tail to the right (high outliers pull mean up). Mean > Median.
  • Skewed left — long tail to the left (low outliers pull mean down). Mean < Median.
Symmetric Skewed right Skewed left _____ __ __ / \ / \____ ___/ \ / \ / \___ __/ \ ───────── ──────────── ─────────────

How to spot skewness on a box plot:

  • Median in the middle of the box, whiskers equal length → symmetric.
  • Median closer to Q1, longer right whisker → skewed right.
  • Median closer to Q3, longer left whisker → skewed left.

B. Mean vs. Median — when to use which

Both measure "the middle." But they react VERY differently to outliers.

Use thisWhenWhy
MeanSymmetric data, no outliersUses every data point — most precise when nothing's pulling it
MedianSkewed data OR has outliersDoesn't get pulled by extreme values — represents "typical" better
Worked example

Books read per week: {0, 24, 1, 4, 5, 2, 5, 4}.

  • Sorted: 0, 1, 2, 4, 4, 5, 5, 24. Mean = 45/8 = 5.625. Median = (4+4)/2 = 4.
  • The 24 is an outlier — pulls the mean way up.
  • The median (4) is more representative of what "typical" looks like.

So: use median.

Question pattern: "Which measure of center is more appropriate?" Look at the data:
  • One big or small value that doesn't fit? → median.
  • Looks pretty even? → mean.

C. Standard Deviation (σ) basics

Standard deviation (σ) measures the typical distance from the mean. Larger σ = data more spread out.

You won't compute σ by hand on this test — but you need to know:

  • σ uses every data point.
  • Outliers strongly affect σ (a single extreme value inflates it).
  • Therefore: σ is best for data without outliers.

D. Standard Deviation vs. IQR — when to use which

Use thisWhenWhy
Standard DeviationNo outliers, fairly symmetricMost precise — uses all data
IQR (Q3 − Q1)Outliers OR skewedIgnores the extreme 25% on each end — robust
The decision pairs up:
  • Mean ↔ Standard Deviation (both use every value, both pulled by outliers).
  • Median ↔ IQR (both ignore extremes, both robust).
If you choose median for the center, you should usually choose IQR for the spread. If mean → σ.

E. The 68-95-99.7 Rule (Normal Distribution)

When a data set is normally distributed (the classic bell curve), three specific percentages of the data fall within standard-deviation bands from the mean:

WithinPercentage
Mean ± 1σ68%
Mean ± 2σ95%
Mean ± 3σ99.7%
.------. .' '. .' '. .' '. .' 68% '. .' ──────────────── '. .' 95% '. .' ────────────────────────── '. _.' 99.7% '._ ──────────────────────────────────────────────── -3σ -2σ -1σ μ +1σ +2σ +3σ
Worked example

Popcorn bucket sizes are normally distributed with mean = 1720 and σ = 20. What percent of buckets contain between 1680 and 1760 pieces?

  1. 1680 = 1720 − 40 = mean − 2σ.
  2. 1760 = 1720 + 40 = mean + 2σ.
  3. So we want the 2σ range → 95%.
Trick to spot which percentage: count how many σ's away from the mean each endpoint is. Then look up the table. Mean ± 1σ → 68; mean ± 2σ → 95; mean ± 3σ → 99.7.

F. Comparing Two Box Plots

When the quiz shows two box plots side by side, scan for:

  1. Median position — which is higher? Which has a more central median (symmetric vs skewed)?
  2. IQR (box width) — which is wider? Which is more consistent?
  3. Whisker length / outliers — which has extreme values?
  4. Min/max range — overall spread.

The "better" or "more consistent" answer usually depends on context (smaller IQR = more consistent). The "higher on average" answer depends on whether you use mean (symmetric) or median (skewed).

G. Practice Problems

Problem 1

A data set is symmetric, with no outliers. Which pair of statistics is best?

  • A. Mean and IQR
  • B. Mean and standard deviation
  • C. Median and standard deviation
  • D. Median and IQR
Show answer
B. Symmetric + no outliers → mean + standard deviation. They pair up (both use every data point).
Problem 2

Books read per week: {0, 24, 1, 4, 5, 2, 5, 4}. Which measure of center and value?

  • A. Median; 2
  • B. Median; 4
  • C. Mean; 2
  • D. Mean; 4
Show answer
B. The 24 is an outlier. Use median. Sorted: 0,1,2,4,4,5,5,24. Median = (4+4)/2 = 4.
Problem 3

Test scores are normally distributed with mean 75 and σ = 5. About what % of scores fall between 65 and 85?

  • A. 68%
  • B. 75%
  • C. 95%
  • D. 99.7%
Show answer
C. 65 = 75 − 2σ; 85 = 75 + 2σ. Mean ± 2σ → 95%.
Problem 4

A toy dog's weight is normally distributed, mean = 8 lb, σ = 1 lb. About 95% of dogs weigh in what range?

  • A. 7-9 lb
  • B. 6-10 lb
  • C. 5-11 lb
  • D. 4-12 lb
Show answer
B. 95% = mean ± 2σ = 8 ± 2 = 6 to 10 lb.
Problem 5

A box plot has min 10.5, Q1 11.5, median 12.5, Q3 13.5, max 15. Which gives a better center, and why?

  • A. Mean, because data is symmetric
  • B. Mean, because data is pulled left
  • C. Median, because data is pulled left
  • D. Median, because data is pulled right
Show answer
D. Median (12.5) is closer to Q1 (11.5) than to Q3 (13.5). Right whisker (15) is also longer than left. Data is skewed RIGHT (pulled to the right by larger values). Use median.
Problem 6

Mid-term scores: 100, 100, 100, 95, 95, 92, 92, 88, 85, 75. Final scores: 98, 95, 93, 91, 88, 82, 78, 78, 65, 60. Which has the larger IQR?

  • A. Mid-term
  • B. Final
  • C. Same IQR
  • D. Not enough info
Show answer
B. Mid-term sorted: 75,85,88,92,92,95,95,100,100,100. Q1 = (88+92)/2 = 90; Q3 = (95+100)/2 = 97.5. IQR = 7.5. Final sorted: 60,65,78,78,82,88,91,93,95,98. Q1 = (78+78)/2 = 78; Q3 = (91+93)/2 = 92. IQR = 14. Final has larger IQR.
Problem 7

A survey says College students: σ = 5.2 (no outliers). High School students: σ = 5.4 (has outliers per Min/Max check). Which spread metric to use for each?

  • A. Both IQR
  • B. Both standard deviation
  • C. College: IQR. High school: standard deviation.
  • D. College: standard deviation. High school: IQR.
Show answer
D. No outliers → σ (College). Has outliers → IQR (High school).
Problem 8

Two box plots: Class — symmetric box, even whiskers. Exam — outlier at the bottom, longer left whisker. Which best describes the medians?

  • A. Exam outlier makes IQR narrower and median higher
  • B. Class data are more evenly spread, pulls its median down
  • C. Class median is lower than exam median
  • D. Class Q3 is lower than exam Q3
Show answer
C. Compare median positions on the number line. If exam median is higher than class median visually, choose C. Hint: medians are LINES inside boxes — read their positions on the x-axis.
Problem 9

Heights: 5 ft 8 in, 5 ft 9 in, 5 ft 10 in, 5 ft 11 in, 6 ft. Distribution is symmetric, no outliers. Best center?

  • A. Mean
  • B. Median
  • C. Mode
  • D. Range
Show answer
A. Symmetric + no outliers → mean. (Range isn't a center; mode is for categorical data.)
Problem 10

Times normally distributed with mean 60, σ = 10. About 99.7% of values are in what range?

  • A. 50-70
  • B. 40-80
  • C. 30-90
  • D. 20-100
Show answer
C. 99.7% = mean ± 3σ = 60 ± 30 = 30 to 90.

H. What to do next

🎯 Key things to remember

  1. Mean ↔ Standard Deviation (both pulled by outliers). Median ↔ IQR (both robust).
  2. Symmetric + no outliers → mean + σ. Skewed or outliers → median + IQR.
  3. 68-95-99.7 rule: within 1σ = 68%, 2σ = 95%, 3σ = 99.7%.
  4. Spot skew on a box plot: median position inside the box + whisker lengths.
  5. Outliers in data: one extreme value pulls the mean way off but barely moves the median.
